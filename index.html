About

Research

Reconfigurable Tendon Driven Continuum Manipulators

Tendon-driven continuum manipulators capable of changing tendon routing during operation. Each spacer disk can rotate around the backbone axis, changing tendon routing locally.

The actuation space is studied by projecting the spatial profile into the Frenet-Serret curvature-torsion frame, yielding qualitative insights that can support a simplified inverse model.

Publications:
  • Actuation Space Reduction to Facilitate Insightful Shape Matching in a Novel Reconfigurable Tendon Driven Continuum Manipulator

    - Accepted for publication in IEEE RoboSoft 2026.

Design Space Exploration for Shape Morphing Metamaterials

Inverse design of shape morphing structures with embedded actuation is a complicated problem, with challenges like proliferation of multiple spurious modes, and difficulty in finidng unique optimal solution. To address this, it is crucial to have an in-depth understanding of the design space under consideration. We developed an enumeration based design space exploration framework, which enumerates important deformed configurations for asymmetric triangular unit meshed mechanisms, following pennate muscle based bio inspired heuristics.

Publications:

    • ASME Journal of Mechanical Design, 2025

  • Enumerating Deformed Configurations of Reconfigurable Meshed Cellular Robots
